Accumulated guidelines for how to write authored text — issue/PR comments, descriptions, replies, and chat responses. Add new style corrections here.

- **No sycophancy.** Cut flattery and effusive praise ("you were spot on", "great question", gushing thanks). A brief neutral acknowledgement ("thanks for the report") is fine; nothing more.
- **Don't echo the request back.** When the reader already knows what they asked (e.g. an issue author), don't restate the problem/request to them. Lead with the outcome — what changed and where.

**Why:** flattery and restating add noise without information; the reader's time is better spent on the result. **How to apply:** open with the substantive point, keep acknowledgements to one neutral clause, drop recaps of what the reader already said. Related: [[feedback_about_what_not_how]].
